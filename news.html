Well-being activities open to all in Luxembourg

11.04.2015 - 14h30 Communication 30 views

Following the huge success of its pilot project with DG TRAD, the Prevention and Well-being at Work Unit has opened its courses in Luxembourg to all: Yoga, pilates, Tai Chi, Mindfulness, and many others. Find an activity that will improve your well-being and register now!

If you are based in Luxembourg and wondered when you would get the same range of well-being activities as colleagues in Brussels, your wait is over. Check out the long list of courses organised by the Prevention and Well-being at Work Unit in Luxembourg - many courses are offered, some of them free and some charged for. All you have to do is choose!

The activities:

  • Anusara Yoga - Anusara is a dynamic form of Hatha yoga which will help you learn how to breathe, relax, and cut down tension.
  • Mindfulness Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) programme - the programme brings together mindfulness meditation and yoga in an 8-week intensive course.
  • Sophrology - a dynamic relaxation method taking both body and mind into account.
  • Pilates - The aim of the class is to gradually build up awareness of the body, strength, and flexibility, by improving posture and co-ordination.
  • Tai Chi - Tai Chi is an ancient Chinese martial art practiced for both its health benefits. It aids in improving balance, reducing blood pressure, and helps you to relax.
  • Qi Gong (course full) - Qi Gong is traditionally viewed as a practice to cultivate and balance qi or "life energy".
  • Integrative yoga therapy (course full) - therapeutic yoga which soothes joints, strengthens the core, and eases tension and pain.
  • Yoga for the office programme (course full) - the Risk Prevention and Well-being at Work Unit offers a free programme of yoga exercise that you can do in the office.

Important information :

Introductory sessions will be organised for some courses until 24 February. Some courses are already full, but places are still available in most - so sign up quickly! You can find all you need to know (description, timetable, price, contacts) on the page  Well-being activities in Luxembourg.
